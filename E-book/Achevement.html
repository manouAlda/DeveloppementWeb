  • Motivation

    • Search for True Love: The novel explores the concept of genuine and lasting love. Readers may be motivated to seek relationships based on sincerity, understanding, and mutual respect.
    • The Importance of Loyalty and Friendship: The friendships and loyalty among characters, such as those between America Singer and her loved ones, inspire readers to value and maintain their own friendships.
    • Courage and Authenticity: The main character, America Singer, is often seen as courageous and authentic, despite facing challenges and social pressures. This can inspire readers to stay true to themselves and defend their beliefs.
    • Questioning Social Structures and Inequalities: The dystopian society in which the story takes place highlights social inequalities and injustices. Readers may be motivated to reflect on current social structures and strive for a fairer and more equitable world.
    • The Importance of Resilience and Perseverance: Characters face various obstacles and need to show resilience and perseverance. This can encourage readers to overcome their own challenges with determination.
    • The Value of Personal Choices: America's journey, which involves making difficult choices about her future and relationships, underscores the importance of personal decisions. Readers may be prompted to carefully consider their own life choices and make decisions aligned with their values and aspirations.

  • Goals

    • Explore the Themes of Love and Relationships: The book deeply examines different dynamics of romantic relationships and friendships. It delves into the complexities of love, loyalty, jealousy, and betrayal, offering readers a reflection on these universal subjects.
    • Criticize Social Structures and Inequalities: Through the dystopian backdrop of Illéa, "The Selection" critiques rigid social structures and class inequalities. The caste system and competition to become the future queen highlight injustices and prompt readers to reflect on similar issues in their own societies.
    • Encourage Independence and Personal Resilience: The protagonist's journey, America Singer, encourages readers to value independence and resilience. Her strong character and ability to overcome challenges inspire readers to remain true to themselves and defend their beliefs, even in difficult circumstances.
    • Promote Reflection on Identity and Personal Choices: America must make significant choices regarding her future, identity, and relationships. The book pushes readers to reflect on their own decisions and understand the importance of making choices that align with their personal values and aspirations.

  • Mission

    • Entertain and Engage Readers: One of the primary objectives of the author is to provide an engaging and entertaining story. With elements of romance, competition, and dystopia, the book is designed to capture a broad audience of young adults, keeping them engaged and encouraging them to follow the series to the end.
    • Exploration of Social and Personal Themes: Kiera Cass uses "The Selection" to explore important themes such as social inequalities, power structures, loyalty, love, and identity quest. By presenting these themes in a fictional setting, she prompts readers to reflect on real and personal issues.
    • Inspiration and Empowerment: Through her main character, America Singer, Cass aims to inspire her readers, especially young women. She highlights values such as authenticity, resilience, courage, and independence, encouraging her readers to believe in themselves and pursue their dreams despite obstacles.
    • Creating an Escape and Dream Space: "The Selection" offers readers an escape from everyday reality, transporting them into a world of romance and luxury mixed with challenges and conflicts. This dream and escape aspect is essential for capturing readers' imaginations and providing them with a respite from the realities of daily life.

  • Strategy

    • Dystopian Framework with Fairy Tale Elements: By combining a dystopian society with fairy tale elements, Kiera Cass creates a world that is both familiar and unique. The dystopian framework appeals to fans of the genre, while fairy tale elements, such as the castle and the competition to become queen, add a touch of magic and romance.
    • Likeable and Relatable Characters: The characters, especially protagonist America Singer, are designed to be both complex and sympathetic. Readers can identify with their struggles, emotions, and personal growth. America is a strong and independent heroine, making her particularly inspiring and appealing.
    • Romantic and Triangular Intrigue: Romance is at the heart of "The Selection," with a love triangle between America, Prince Maxon, and her former love Aspen. This dynamic creates constant emotional tension and keeps readers invested in the resolution of the relationships.
    • Suspense and Competition: The competition itself is a constant source of suspense. The progressive eliminations of the contestants and the challenges they must overcome add pace and tension that keep readers engaged.
    • Universal Themes: The themes addressed in the book, such as love, loyalty, social injustice, and self-discovery, are universal and resonate with a wide audience. These themes allow readers to connect with the story on a deeper level.
